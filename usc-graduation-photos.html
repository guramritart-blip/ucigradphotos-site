Campus Locations

Best Photo Spots at USC

USC's campus is rich with history and stunning architecture. Here are the locations I recommend most for your Trojan graduation portraits.

Mudd Hall

Mudd Hall of Philosophy is one of USC's most beautiful hidden gems. The Romanesque architecture, arched doorways, and lush surrounding gardens create a dramatic, old-world feel that photographs beautifully in any light.

Tommy Trojan

The Tommy Trojan statue at the center of campus is the most iconic symbol of USC. The bronze statue surrounded by the Trojan Shrine creates a powerful, recognizable backdrop that every Trojan graduate wants in their gallery.

Traveler Horse Statue

The Traveler statue near the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum captures the spirit of USC athletics and tradition. The dynamic bronze horse and rider make for a bold, energetic backdrop that stands out from typical graduation portraits.

Doheny Memorial Library

Doheny Library is the architectural crown jewel of USC. The Romanesque facade, arched entrance, and grand staircase offer endless composition options. Whether shooting from the front steps or framing through the pillars, Doheny provides a timeless feel.

Bovard Auditorium

The red brick exterior and white columns of Bovard Auditorium give your photos a classic collegiate aesthetic. This building sits prominently on Trousdale Parkway, and the long walkway leading up to it creates a dramatic approach shot.

Steps of Troy

The wide, sweeping staircase at the heart of campus is a classic USC graduation photo spot. The symmetrical steps and surrounding architecture create a grand, cinematic composition that works beautifully for both solo and group portraits.

Shumway Fountain

The Patsy and Forrest Shumway Fountain near Alumni Park is a stunning water feature surrounded by lush landscaping. The cascading water and open setting create a fresh, vibrant backdrop that adds movement and energy to your graduation portraits.

Capturing the Trojan Experience

Guramrit Malhotra / G

The University of Southern California is one of the most photographed campuses in the country, and for good reason. From the Romanesque columns of Doheny Memorial Library to the bronze glow of Tommy Trojan at golden hour, USC offers backdrops that are both grand and deeply personal. I have photographed dozens of Trojan graduates across every corner of the campus, and I know how to use those iconic settings to create portraits that stand out.

Every USC session starts with a plan. Before your shoot day, I will discuss your preferred locations, the number of outfit changes, and any group shots you would like to include. On the day of, I arrive with professional off-camera lighting equipment that gives your photos a polished, editorial quality you simply cannot achieve with natural light alone.

Whether you are finishing your undergraduate degree, completing your MBA at Marshall, or defending your dissertation, your USC graduation marks years of dedication. My goal is to match that energy with photos that feel as significant as the achievement itself. All raw, unedited photos are sent within 24 hours. Edited and retouched images follow in 7 to 10 days.
